## How is the L1 seller determined on the GeM Portal?

On the **Government e-Marketplace (GeM)**, the **L1 (Lowest Bidder 1)** represents the seller who has offered the lowest financial bid among all technically qualified bidders. Securing L1 status is critical because, in most public procurements, the contract is automatically awarded to the L1 supplier.

Understanding how the system evaluates bids can help you structure your pricing to win tenders while maintaining healthy profit margins.

---

## What is the difference between Single Item and Bunched Bid L1?

The L1 calculation differs depending on the type of tender:

* **Single Item Bids**: The evaluation is straightforward. The seller with the lowest price for the specific product/service is declared L1.
* **Bunched Bids (Multi-item)**: The buyer bunches multiple related products (e.g., Desktop, Printer, and UPS) into a single tender. Here, GeM calculates L1 based on the **Total Price of the entire bunch**.
  * *Important Tip*: You do not need to be the cheapest on every item. As long as your **cumulative total** for all items is the lowest, you will win L1.

## How does L1 price matching work?

Under specific government directives (like the MSME Procurement Policy and Make in India Order), buyers are allowed to offer price matching opportunities:

1. **MSME Purchase Preference**: If an MSME seller falls within the price band of **L1 + 15%**, they are allowed to match the L1 price (if the L1 seller is a non-MSME). Upon matching, they are awarded a portion of the order (typically 25%).
2. **Local Supplier Preference**: Similar matching options exist for Class-1 Local Suppliers (MII) who fall within **L1 + 20%** of a non-local L1 bidder.

## How to optimize your catalog for L1 bids?

* **Factor in Delivery & Installation**: Always include transport, local taxes, and warranty costs inside the unit price. Underestimating logistics is a common reason why L1 contracts become unprofitable.
* **Competitor Benchmarking**: Use helper extensions like **GeM Sahayak** to extract past L1 contract values for similar items in your target location (e.g., Bhopal, Indore). Knowing what prices won tenders last year gives you a highly accurate baseline.
* **Automate Alerts**: Keep track of the active bids pool in your region. If you bid early and monitor competitor catalog updates, you can make strategic adjustments before the financial bidding window closes.
